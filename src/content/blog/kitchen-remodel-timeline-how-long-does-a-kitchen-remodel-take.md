## How Long Does a Kitchen Remodel Take?

As mentioned, it's difficult to nail down a kitchen remodel timeline without understanding what's happening in the renovation. On average, a full-scale kitchen remodel can take [anywhere from 6-12 weeks](https://www.hgtv.com/design/remodel/kitchen-remodel/how-long-does-it-typically-take-remodel-kitchen).

Smaller-scale remodels with straightforward updates may only take a few weeks, while full-scale rebuilds with unforeseen issues can take many months. The best way to understand how long your kitchen remodel might take is to look at a typical kitchen remodel timeline.

## Kitchen Remodel Timeline

A standard kitchen remodel timeline can be broken down into four stages. First, you've got the planning and design phase. This is followed by the demolition of the old kitchen components.

Once the old kitchen is gone, the building team will undergo the construction and installation phase. Finally, you'll put the finishing touches on the new kitchen and your remodel will be complete.

![a noblessa modular kitchen](/assets/images/blog/december2024/kitchen-remodel-timeline-how-long-does-a-kitchen-remodel-take-3.jpg)

### Planning and Design

Before you break ground and start tearing your old kitchen apart, you need a plan for your new kitchen. This process involves planning the layout, choosing the materials, and finalizing the details of your design.

Most homeowners spend months, if not years, dreaming up a new kitchen. There's no need to rush this process, but if you're working with a reputable designer, they can use their expertise to help you [put together a viable design](https://noblessa-usa.com/partner-resources/) much faster than you could by yourself.

Part of the planning phase will involve getting your ducks in a row. You'll need to have your materials picked out and your laborers scheduled. Any backups in this realm can sometimes cause lengthy delays before you start demolition.

![a noblessa modular kitchen](/assets/images/blog/december2024/kitchen-remodel-timeline-how-long-does-a-kitchen-remodel-take-4.jpg)

### Demolition

Demolition is usually the [most straightforward aspect](https://www.dumpsters.com/blog/how-to-demo-a-kitchen) of a kitchen remodeling project, barring any hidden issues in the home. It involves tearing out old cabinets, pulling up countertops and flooring, and removing old appliances – this part usually takes about a week.

If anything needs to be done with the plumbing or electrical systems, you'll need to call in the necessary professionals. This will add time to your renovation, but it pays off immensely in the long run because you'll avoid dealing with those issues later on.

![a noblessa modular kitchen](/assets/images/blog/december2024/kitchen-remodel-timeline-how-long-does-a-kitchen-remodel-take-5.jpg)

### Construction and Installation

The construction and installation process makes up the bulk of your kitchen remodel timeline. It'll start with any plumbing, electrical work, and framing that needs to be completed. Then, you'll install the cabinets, countertops, flooring, and backsplashes.

Once the structure of the kitchen is in place, you can install the new (or [reinstall the old](https://www.architecturaldigest.com/story/the-ultimate-guide-on-when-to-replace-your-household-appliances)) appliances and reconnect the plumbing/electricity. As the most labor-intensive part of the remodel, this can take several weeks.

![a noblessa modular kitchen](/assets/images/blog/december2024/kitchen-remodel-timeline-how-long-does-a-kitchen-remodel-take-6.jpg)

### Finishing Touches

The final phase involves putting the finishing touches on your kitchen. It includes painting, trim work, organizing extra storage, and final inspections.

## Kitchen Remodel Tips

Our best advice for pulling off a kitchen remodel is to start planning and designing early. Don't think about the timeline when you're working on your design. If you take your time during this phase, you'll be able to lay out a plan that makes the rest of the project simple and quick.

When planning, account for storage, functionality, and durability. Choose quality components made from sustainable materials and ensure you're happy with how things look.

By working with a designer, you can take much of the work off your plate. Once you've agreed upon a design, they'll handle things like ordering materials and organizing labor. Great designers will offer clear communication and see your project through until the end.

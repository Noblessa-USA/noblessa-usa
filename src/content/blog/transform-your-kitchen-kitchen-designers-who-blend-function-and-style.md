## Sustainability in Kitchen Design

Modern luxury kitchens seamlessly incorporate sustainability. You can find a range of recycled, renewable, and responsibly sourced materials that add beauty to your space.

Kitchen designers from noblessa have a proven commitment to sustainability, including national and international certifications and awards.

A focus on the environment goes beyond materials. It also includes the means of production and product shipping. International standards for environmental and energy management include [ISO 14001](https://www.epa.gov/ems/frequent-questions-about-environmental-management-systems) and ISO 50001.

Even shipping practices seek to minimize the carbon footprint.

![Sustainable kitchen design](/assets/images/blog/august2024/transform-your-kitchen-kitchen-designers-who-blend-function-and-style-2.jpeg)

### Multipurpose Islands

Islands continue to be relevant in sophisticated kitchen design. Today's islands offer more functionality and style options.

Your kitchen designer will help you consider how you want to use the island. Far from the flat countertops of the past, islands can now feature multiple levels and options like:

- Prep sink
- Cooktop
- Second dishwasher
- Warming drawer
- Integrated wine storage
- Open display shelves

An island remains a practical way to add casual seating. Larger islands with wraparound seating are increasingly common.

![Multipurpose kitchen island](/assets/images/blog/august2024/transform-your-kitchen-kitchen-designers-who-blend-function-and-style-3.jpeg)

### Triangle vs. Workstation Kitchen Layout

Traditional kitchen design was based on a work triangle with the stove, sink, and refrigerator at the three points. This kitchen layout still works well for [smaller spaces](https://noblessa-usa.com/blog/small-kitchen-ideas-designing-for-storage/) with a single cook.

Your kitchen designer may recommend a workstation approach for larger kitchens that need to accommodate more people. This layout divides the kitchen into areas dedicated to different tasks. It minimizes the need to move continuously throughout the kitchen.

Common workstations include:

- Food prep
- Cooking
- Storage
- Clean up

Each task station is usually based around an appliance.

Depending on your lifestyle, you may want stations devoted to specialized tasks like canning. If someone in your household starts the day with coffee or a smoothie, you may wish to have an area dedicated to the coffee maker or blender. You can store all the necessary items like mugs or glasses close by.

![Workstation kitchen layout](/assets/images/blog/august2024/transform-your-kitchen-kitchen-designers-who-blend-function-and-style-4.jpeg)

### Traffic Flow in the Kitchen

Good traffic flow is essential for getting the most out of your kitchen. Kitchen designers can help you see how people currently move through the system. You don't want the cook constantly running into people coming and going.

Minor adjustments can have a large impact. For example, if traffic flows through the kitchen to another room, be sure the cooktop isn't in that busy path. An island can separate the workstations from the social part of the kitchen.

![Kitchen traffic flow design](/assets/images/blog/august2024/transform-your-kitchen-kitchen-designers-who-blend-function-and-style-5.jpg)

## Fresh Cabinetry

The cabinets are one of the most important choices for your kitchen renovation. Quality cabinets like noblessa blend artistry and functionality.

### Wood

Stained wood is a trending interior design choice. Wood has a natural beauty and warmth. It gives your kitchen an intimate, cozy feel.

![Wood kitchen cabinets](/assets/images/blog/august2024/transform-your-kitchen-kitchen-designers-who-blend-function-and-style-6.jpg)

### Color Contrasts

You can add personality with colored cabinets. If you don't want traditional white, the palette is much wider. Black or gray adds cool sophistication to a modern kitchen. Cranberry red, emerald green, or navy blue make a dramatic statement. Darker cabinets work better in a larger space. If necessary, your kitchen designer will help you find ways to balance them out in a smaller space.

### Hardware

Cabinet hardware is another opportunity to personalize your kitchen. From sleek minimalist pieces to hardware that makes a bold statement, you can find the right look for your space.

Metal options include brass, copper, bronze, and chrome. You can mix these metals for an eclectic look. Matte black finishes are another growing trend.

For a truly minimalist look, you can use concealed hardware. Hidden hardware creates a streamlined look, letting other elements of your kitchen stand out.

![Kitchen cabinet hardware](/assets/images/blog/august2024/transform-your-kitchen-kitchen-designers-who-blend-function-and-style-7.png)

### Storage

Functional storage is an essential part of modern kitchen design. Lower drawers and open shelving are two options to consider.

Drawers are increasingly popular instead of lower cabinets. A variety of inserts makes the drawers more versatile, such as:

- Spice drawer
- Utensil bin pull-out or caddy
- Tray divider
- Cutlery divider
- Stemware rack

You have easy access to your items while maintaining a clean aesthetic. Open shelving can also be an attractive storage solution. You can display glassware or dishes as well as highlight art and decor. Open shelving helps break up entire walls covered in cabinetry, which will improve the visual interest.

![Functional kitchen storage](/assets/images/blog/august2024/transform-your-kitchen-kitchen-designers-who-blend-function-and-style-8.jpg)

## Layered Lighting

Kitchens need a blend of [four types of lighting](https://www.modern.place/what-are-the-4-types-of-light/):

- Task
- Ambient
- Accent
- Decorative

Bright daylight bulbs are common for task lighting. Adding layers with warmer lighting styles, like pendants over an island, will give you a more enjoyable overall result.

Integrated lighting in cabinets and open shelving provides light and ambiance. It provides illumination when natural light is limited, like underneath cabinets. It can highlight areas like the countertop or backsplash.

![Layered kitchen lighting](/assets/images/blog/august2024/transform-your-kitchen-kitchen-designers-who-blend-function-and-style-9.jpeg)

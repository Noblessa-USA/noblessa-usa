## What does a kitchen island add to your space?

A kitchen island is more than extra counter space. In the right plan, it can support multiple needs at once:

* Meal prep and serving
* Casual seating and conversation
* Extra storage to keep counters clear
* A visual centerpiece that anchors the room

The key is matching the island to how you actually use your kitchen, not just how it looks in photos.

## How big should a kitchen island be?

The best island size depends on your layout, traffic flow, and how many people use the kitchen at the same time. Instead of starting with a number, start with clearances. You want comfortable walking space around the island so it does not become an obstacle. Once the space feels right, your designer can help shape the island footprint to support seating, storage, and prep space without crowding the room.

## Which island style fits your lifestyle?

There is no one “perfect” island - there is the island that is perfect for you. Here are a few evergreen directions that work across many homes:

**The prep-first island**

Great for people who cook often. Prioritize a generous work surface and keep the surrounding storage efficient so tools and ingredients are always within reach.

**The gather-around island**

Ideal for entertaining and busy households. Add comfortable seating and make the island a place where guests can chat, kids can snack, and life can happen while meals come together.

**The storage-forward island**

Perfect when you want a cleaner look. More storage in the island means fewer items on the counters, which helps the whole kitchen feel calmer and more elevated.
